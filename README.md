### About me

I'm an Electrical and Electronics Engineer working on Intel's **Xeon microprocessor product development** team, where I focus on Design for Test (DFT), reset validation, and software-driven silicon debug. My day-to-day sits at the intersection of low-level hardware behaviour and the automation software that makes large-scale silicon bring-up tractable.

Outside Intel I build products end-to-end — from database schema to deployed frontend — driven by the same philosophy: systems should be resilient, maintainable, and fast.

- Scan chain integration, multi-domain reset validation, early silicon bring-up
- Automation tooling in **C++, Python, Verilog/SystemVerilog, and Tessent**
- Former student worker on Xeon platform test programs
- Contributed to **AREX** (space instrumentation research) and an **IEEE-published** Braille electronic board game
- B.S. Electrical & Electronics Engineering — Instituto Tecnológico de Costa Rica (ITCR)

### Projects

#### ArchivoRunner
> Race photography platform for trail and road events across Costa Rica.

Runners find their race photos by bib number or face search. Organizers get a full admin dashboard with sponsor tiers, pre-launch race pages, interactive GPX route maps with elevation profiles, and a YOLO-based bib detection pipeline that processes thousands of photos per race.

#### Popshot
> Selfie-based event photo search — find yourself in any crowd.

Upload a selfie at an event and the platform surfaces every photo you appear in using face embeddings. Built to scale across multiple concurrent events with a clean mobile-first UI.
